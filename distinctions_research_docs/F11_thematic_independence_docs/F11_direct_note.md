## Main result
The direct batch1 motif-origin table contains **102** boundary/opening/naming-family motif events.

Origin split:
- cross-source transfer: **39 / 102 = 38.2%**
- endogenous: **32 / 102 = 31.4%**
- could be source continuation: **31 / 102 = 30.4%**

So **69.6%** of motif events are **not** explainable by simple continuation of the currently active source.

## Epoch structure
- E1 shows all three origin modes present.
- E2 contains **0** source-continuation cases in the direct table; motif events there are split between cross-source transfer and endogenous emergence.
- E3 again shows all three modes, with cross-source transfer the largest single bucket.

## Active-source structure
- Under **Finnegans Wake**, direct source-continuation is **0 / 31** in this table.
- Under the mixed **Germinal + War and Peace** interval, all three modes occur at appreciable rates.
- Under **Russell**, cross-source transfer remains the largest single bucket in the direct table.

## Why this matters
This direct batch1 table is stronger than the fallback public-archive reconstruction because the origin labels are already adjudicated at event level. It supports the evidence-only statement that the boundary/opening/naming attractor is frequently expressed in ways that are not reducible to simple continuation of the current source stream.
