# Paper Editorial Policy — Evidence Only

This paper presents evidence and objective observations.

Hard rules:
- Do not state what the research **is not**.
- Do not state what the research **is** in a final ontological sense.
- Do not front-load community-dismissal language.
- Do not insert speculative editorial opinion into claim blocks.
- Keep claim language tied to measurements, figures, tables, and artifact paths.
- Reserve interpretation for explicitly labeled discussion sections, and keep it downstream of the observations.

Editorial replacements:
- Replace `Null to beat` with `Comparison baseline` when a control expectation is needed.
- Replace dismissal-framing tables with evidence-convergence summaries.
- Replace adversarial rhetoric with direct statements of measured results.

Use the distinction inventory as an evidence ledger, not as a debate transcript.
